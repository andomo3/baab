The catch

.hero-widget-row is grid-template-columns: 1fr 1fr (styles.css:825) with exactly two unnamed <div> children (index.html:83–118). Delete the shelf and the terminal keeps its half; the other half goes blank. So this change can’t stop at removal — the row has to be re-conceived.

Also load-bearing: animations.js:156 reveals .hero-widget-row > div by that exact structural selector, and .tile is in the same list.

Three things worth knowing before you cut

Verified against the source
Safe

The project data isn’t going anywhere

PROJECT_FILES (app.js:5) also drives the Projects IDE — file tree, tabs, prev/next. Only tile() (app.js:205) and the two append lines at app.js:222–223 become dead. The array stays.

Intended

Home loses its only imagery

The four tiles carry the generative preview art (styles.css:502–625, one treatment per project ID). You’ve called this a gain, not a loss: the descriptions and results should carry the projects. Home becomes a headshot, a prompt, and a record.

Drive-by

There’s already a dead section below

The heatmap block at index.html:173–198 carries style="display:none;". If you’re trimming home, delete it in the same pass rather than leaving hidden markup behind.

Why C over A

A is the safe answer and it would ship today. But the shelf’s removal is really an admission that home was trying to be a directory. Once you accept that, home’s job is: say who you are, hand over one memorable interaction, show the track record. C is the layout that says that out loud — the dark island stops being a banner and becomes the whole introduction.

If you’d rather not touch the hero yet, A is a clean stepping stone; C stays reachable from it.

Decided

Your calls, folded into the build above

No heading above the terminal

The “Now Playing / A music CLI” .section-head comes out entirely — it would have needed a dark-palette variant to survive inside the hero anyway. The shell’s own title bar, abba@portfolio — zsh, does the labeling. One element, one job.

Hero stats trimmed to two

Keeping 6 countries lived and 5 languages. “6 shipped projects” is already the Projects page’s whole argument, and “2 internships” is on the résumé and the timeline directly below. The two that stay are the only ones the rest of the page never says. Say the word if you’d rather swap one for internships.

Project imagery pulled everywhere

You were right that the IDE never showed it — I checked: preview and every .art node are rendered only inside tile() (app.js:210–211), which is home-only. So deleting the tile CSS removes the art from the whole site in one move. Descriptions and results carry the projects.

One consequence: perchance.webp and altura.webp become unreferenced, and the two preview: fields in PROJECT_FILES go dead. Those were compressed two commits ago — I’ll leave the files on disk and flag them rather than deleting your assets.

What building it surfaced

Found by driving the real page, not by reading the diff
Fixed

The prompt was landing below the fold

First build: on a 1440×900 laptop only 164px of the 407px shell was visible — the payoff was off-screen, which defeats the whole argument for C. Tightened the hero (padding 72/80 → 52/56, name clamp 88 → 76px, photo 340 → 280px, row margin 56 → 34px). Now 236px shows, with a deliberate scroll cue.

Fixed

Phone spent 155px on three stacked buttons

The ≤767px rule stacked all three actions full-width. Now “View Projects” spans the row and Resume + LinkedIn share the line below it. Terminal visibility on a 390px phone went 101px → 171px of 281px.

Aligned

The photo sat 10px inside the shell’s edge

.hero-photo-wrap centred its image in a wider column. Switched to flex-end so the headshot’s right edge and the terminal’s now form one line.

Verified end to end

Drove the real page in Chromium at 1440×900 and 390×844: no console errors, no horizontal overflow at either width. Typed cd projects into the live CLI — it navigates, and the Projects IDE renders all six entries with tree, tabs, and STAR cards intact. Confirmed .hero-terminal-row resolves to opacity: 1 under prefers-reduced-motion: reduce.

Also removed in passing: the dead display:none heatmap — its markup, its ~90 lines of JS (including a GitHub fetch that never ran), and its CSS.
